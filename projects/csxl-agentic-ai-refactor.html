CSXL Agentic AI Refactor

Refactoring UNC's Computer Science Experience Labs platform to support agentic AI-driven development workflows, structured acceptance testing, and modern repository standards.

View Live Project →

Overview

The CSXL (Computer Science Experience Labs) platform is UNC Chapel Hill's hub for CS students — connecting them to office hours, equipment loans, study spaces, and community events. This project, completed as part of COMP 523 (Software Engineering Laboratory), involves a large-scale refactor of the existing codebase to integrate agentic AI development workflows.

The refactor introduces acceptance testing pipelines so that AI agents can be reliably used to propose and validate changes to the platform. It also brings the repository up to modern standards with improved CI/CD integration, clearer contribution guidelines, and better separation of concerns across the stack.

  • Refactored backend service layer to support agentic AI interaction patterns
  • Designed and implemented structured acceptance test suites for key user flows
  • Updated repository standards: linting, branching conventions, PR templates
  • Collaborated with a team of 4 engineers across a full semester sprint cycle

Tech Stack

Built on the existing CSXL stack with additions to support AI-driven workflows and testing infrastructure.
